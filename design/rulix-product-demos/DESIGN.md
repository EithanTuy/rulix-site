# Rulix Product Demo Visual Identity

## Style Prompt
Premium compliance product proof for export-control memo review. The clips should feel like a secure review room: dark graphite canvas, sharp white product screenshots, restrained cyan accents, and amber/red only for real review-risk states. Motion should clarify the sequence from memo intake to evidence mapping to human decision and exportable audit record.

## Colors
- Background: `#050b12`
- Raised panel: `#081827`
- Product frame: `#e8eef5`
- Primary text: `#f6fbff`
- Secondary text: `#a9bad0`
- Accent cyan: `#0ed5dc`
- Evidence green: `#7fd17b`
- Review amber: `#f2a51a`
- Conflict red: `#ff6773`

## Typography
- Headlines: `Arial`, weight 760-860.
- Body and labels: `Arial`, weight 380-620.
- Data/time markers: `Consolas`, tabular numerals.

## Motion Rules
- Start every scene with a 0.15-0.25s breath before major entrances.
- Use blur crossfades between related product states.
- Animate the product screenshot from scale and opacity, not fake app controls.
- Callouts should enter after the screenshot, with short staggered motion.
- No infinite loops inside the HyperFrames timeline. The website may loop rendered MP4s.

## What Not To Do
- Do not invent product UI beyond concise overlays and callouts.
- Do not show passwords, invite tokens, or customer data.
- Do not claim certifications, legal guarantees, or autonomous regulatory decisions.
- Do not use playful motion, sparkles, or unsupported metrics.
